Parking Hero

Usability Testing Protocol

1 Participant Recruitment

Target users: Students who park on campus regularly

Recruitment criteria:

  • Students (primary users)
  • Daily or frequent parkers (3-4x per week)
  • Android or iPhone users
  • No prior parking app experience (preferred)
  • Not involved in the project development

2 Team Member Roles

Facilitator (Olivia Foster): Introduces tasks, guides session, asks questions
Observer (Ethan Demarest): Takes detailed notes, records times and errors

3 Testing Tasks

Task 1: Find a Parking Spot (Simple)

Scenario: You've just arrived on campus and need to find an available parking spot. Use the app to locate where you can park.

Success Criteria: Participant successfully views available parking spots and begins navigation.

Expected Time: 2-4 minutes

Task 2: Campus Event Parking (Moderate)

Scenario: There's a basketball game on campus today and parking is limited. Use the app to find parking during this busy time.

Success Criteria: Participant successfully finds a spot from notifications during the busy campus event parking scenario and navigates to spot.

Expected Time: 3-5 minutes

Task 3: Emergency Navigation to Vehicle (Complex)

Scenario: There's an emergency alert on campus. Use the app to get directions back to your parked spot.

Success Criteria: Participant successfully gets directions back to the parking spot.

Expected Time: 2-4 minutes

4 Metrics and Questionnaires

Required Metrics

  • Task Completion Rate: Calculated as (completed tasks / total tasks) √ó 100%
  • Task Completion Time: Recording in minutes for each task
  • Error Count: Critical errors (prevent completion) and non-critical errors (recovered)
  • SEQ (Single Ease Question): 7-point scale after each task (1=Very Difficult, 7=Very Easy)
  • SUS (System Usability Scale): 10-question survey after all tasks completed

5 Data Collection During Test

Record for each participant:

  • Participant background (student/staff, parking frequency, phone type)
  • Start time and end time for each task
  • Task completion: Success, Partial Success, or Failure
  • Number of critical and non-critical errors
  • SEQ rating immediately after each task
  • Observer notes on behavior, comments, and issues
  • Photos of participant during testing (with permission)

6 Informed Consent Form

Participants must sign before testing begins. Include:

  • Study purpose: Evaluate usability of Campus Parking Application
  • Procedures: 3 tasks, approximately 15-20 minutes total
  • Voluntary participation: Can withdraw at any time
  • Confidentiality: Data identified by participant number only
  • Contact information: Principal investigator details
  • Signature lines: Participant name, signature, and date

7 Debriefing Questions

Ask after completing all tasks and SUS questionnaire:

  1. What did you like most about the app?
  2. What did you like least or find most frustrating?
  3. Was there anything that surprised you or didn't work as expected?
  4. What features would you add or change?
  5. Would you use this app? Why or why not?
  6. Any other comments or suggestions?
